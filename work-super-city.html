A City That Listens

Super City is a permanent interactive exhibit at Hubei Science and Technology Museum, designed and built by Low-Tech Art Lab. The work imagines the city not as architecture, but as a living organism that responds to visitor presence — a small urban ecology shaped by sound, light, and movement.

This project was developed under the direction of Low-Tech Art Lab, founded by artist Zheng Da. As a project participant, I contributed to the creative development and on-site implementation of the work.

An Interactive Urban Experience

The installation invites visitors to become part of the city's nervous system. Sound, motion, and proximity all become inputs — light patterns ripple across the modular structures, ambient soundscapes shift, and the city responds in continuously evolving ways.

As a permanent exhibit in a science museum, Super City serves not only as an artwork but as a public interface — where children, families, and curious visitors discover that the boundary between observer and city is porous.

The work draws on Low-Tech Art Lab's signature approach: combining custom-designed integrated circuit boards, modular hardware systems, and inter-disciplinary creative methods. From DEMO prototype to final implementation, every layer of the installation is purpose-built.

— System Logic —

The Logic Beneath

Underneath the visible city is a layered logic — sensor inputs feeding micro-controllers, micro-controllers feeding a real-time visualization engine, the engine feeding ambient sound and light. Low-Tech Art Lab's custom PCBs handle the conversion between physical signal and digital response.

— Installation Details —

Close-up

Construction, lighting modules, mechanical detailing, sensor integration — the close-up reveals how the city is built.
